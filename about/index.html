Welcome to Leo’s Cyber Lab

Hi there! I’m Leo (levu12), and this is my cybersecurity blog focused on CTF challenges, OSINT investigations, and digital forensics research.

About This Blog

Leo’s Cyber Lab is dedicated to sharing practical knowledge and detailed writeups in cybersecurity. Whether you’re a fellow CTF player, OSINT practitioner, or someone interested in digital investigations, you’ll find comprehensive guides and real-world case studies here.

What You’ll Find Here

  • CTF Writeups: Detailed solutions for web exploitation, cryptography, forensics, and reverse engineering challenges
  • OSINT Investigations: Real-world techniques for open source intelligence gathering and analysis
  • Digital Forensics: Practical approaches to examining digital evidence and conducting investigations
  • Tool Tutorials: Step-by-step guides for essential cybersecurity tools and frameworks

My Background

I’m a passionate cybersecurity researcher and CTF competitor with experience across multiple domains of information security. I believe in the power of knowledge sharing and helping others develop the skills needed to secure our digital world.

Through this blog, I aim to provide detailed, educational content that helps both newcomers and experienced practitioners improve their cybersecurity skills.

Areas of Expertise

  • Web Application Security: SQL injection, XSS, authentication bypass, file upload vulnerabilities
  • OSINT & Threat Intelligence: Social media investigation, infrastructure analysis, digital footprinting
  • Digital Forensics: Memory analysis, network forensics, malware investigation
  • CTF Competitions: Active participant with experience in major international competitions

Philosophy

I believe cybersecurity is a team sport. By sharing detailed writeups, methodologies, and lessons learned, we can all help make the digital world more secure. Every challenge solved and every technique mastered contributes to our collective defense against cyber threats.

My approach emphasizes:

  • Methodical investigation with clear documentation
  • Ethical responsibility in all security research
  • Continuous learning and skill development
  • Community collaboration and knowledge sharing
